Reporting Texas obtained data from the University of Texas at Austin on the 110 people on campus who are citizens of the countries listed in President Trump's executive order.

Iranian graduate students in science, technology, engineering and math fields would be overwhelmingly affected.

Next: National trends →

These findings reflect national trends: More than 12,000 Iranian students enrolled at U.S. universities in the 2015-2016 academic year — the highest number since the Iranian Revolution in 1979, when Iran was the top country of origin for international students in the U.S.

More than three-quarters of Iranian students nationally are in STEM fields, and Texas has the second-highest population of STEM students in the country.

Most on student visas
